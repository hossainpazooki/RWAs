REGULATION (EU) 2023/1114 OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL
of 31 May 2023
on markets in crypto-assets (MiCA)

================================================================================
TITLE III - ASSET-REFERENCED TOKENS
================================================================================

Chapter 1 - Authorisation to offer asset-referenced tokens to the public

Article 36 - Authorisation

1. No person shall make a public offer of an asset-referenced token in the Union or seek the admission to trading of such a crypto-asset unless that person is the issuer of such asset-referenced token and:

(a) is a legal person or other undertaking that is established in the Union and has been authorised in accordance with Article 21 by the competent authority of its home Member State; and

(b) complies with the requirements set out in this Title.

2. Paragraph 1 shall not apply to issuers of asset-referenced tokens that are:

(a) a credit institution authorised under Directive 2013/36/EU;

(b) exempted pursuant to Article 17;

(c) natural persons.

3. The authorisation referred to in paragraph 1 shall be valid for the entire Union and shall allow the issuer to offer asset-referenced tokens to the public in the Union and to seek admission of such crypto-assets to trading.

4. The authorisation granted under this Article shall be without prejudice to the obligation to obtain any other authorisation required under Union or national law.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 37 - Custody of reserve assets

1. Issuers of asset-referenced tokens shall establish, maintain and implement custody policies, procedures and contractual arrangements that ensure at all times that:

(a) the reserve assets are not encumbered nor pledged as collateral;

(b) the reserve assets are held by credit institutions or crypto-asset service providers authorised for the custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients, in accordance with Article 75.

2. The custody arrangements shall ensure that the reserve assets are segregated from the issuer's own assets and from assets of the custodian.

3. Issuers shall ensure that custodians are appointed on the basis of their expertise and market reputation, taking into account their capital position and their ability to ensure the safekeeping of the reserve assets.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 38 - Reserve of assets

1. Issuers of asset-referenced tokens shall at all times maintain a reserve of assets.

2. The reserve of assets shall:

(a) be composed of the assets referenced by the asset-referenced token;

(b) be held by authorised custodians in accordance with Article 37;

(c) be segregated from the issuer's own funds and managed separately;

(d) not be used by the issuer for investments;

(e) be managed in a prudent manner to ensure that the value of the reserve assets is at least equal to the value of the outstanding asset-referenced tokens.

3. The reserve assets shall be composed of assets that are highly liquid with minimal market and credit risk. The composition shall allow for quick liquidation with minimal adverse price effect.

4. Where the asset-referenced token references a single official currency, at least 30% of the reserve shall be held in deposits in credit institutions, with no more than 5% held in any single institution.

5. Where the asset-referenced token references multiple official currencies or a basket of assets, the reserve shall be composed in a manner that reflects the composition of the reference basket.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Chapter 3 - Significant asset-referenced tokens

Article 43 - Classification of asset-referenced tokens as significant

1. The EBA shall classify an asset-referenced token as significant where at least three of the following criteria are met:

(a) the size of the customer base of the promoters, the shareholders of the issuer or of any third-party entity exceeds 10 million;

(b) the value of the asset-referenced tokens issued or the market capitalisation exceeds EUR 5 000 000 000;

(c) the average number and value of transactions exceeds 2.5 million transactions and EUR 500 000 000 per day during the relevant period;

(d) the issuer of the asset-referenced token is a provider of core platform services designated as a gatekeeper in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2022/1925;

(e) the significance of the activities of the issuer of the asset-referenced token on an international scale;

(f) the interconnectedness of the asset-referenced token with the financial system.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 45 - Specific additional obligations for issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens

1. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall be subject to supervision by the EBA instead of the competent authority of their home Member State.

2. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall adopt, implement and maintain:

(a) a remuneration policy that promotes sound and effective risk management and does not create incentives to relax risk standards;

(b) arrangements to ensure the continuity and regularity in the performance of their services and activities.

3. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall ensure that their internal control mechanisms and risk assessment procedures are sufficiently robust.

4. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall have in place:

(a) liquidity management policies and procedures to ensure that the reserve assets have a resilient liquidity profile enabling normal operation under various stress scenarios;

(b) conduct regular stress tests addressing liquidity risks, market risks and credit risks.

5. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall hold own funds equal to the higher of:

(a) EUR 350 000;

(b) 3% of the average amount of the reserve of assets.

6. The EBA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify further the liquidity requirements and the own funds requirements applicable to issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 46 - Recovery plan

1. Issuers of significant asset-referenced tokens shall draw up and maintain a recovery plan providing for measures to be taken by the issuer to restore compliance with the requirements applicable to the reserve of assets in cases where the issuer fails to comply with those requirements.

2. The recovery plan shall include appropriate conditions and procedures to ensure the timely implementation of recovery actions.

================================================================================
TITLE IV - E-MONEY TOKENS
================================================================================

Article 48 - Authorisation

1. No person shall make a public offer of an e-money token in the Union or seek the admission to trading of such e-money token unless that person is the issuer of such e-money token and:

(a) is authorised as a credit institution under Directive 2013/36/EU, or as an electronic money institution under Directive 2009/110/EC; and

(b) complies with the requirements set out in this Title and in Directive 2009/110/EC.

2. E-money tokens shall be deemed to be electronic money within the meaning of Article 2, point (2), of Directive 2009/110/EC.

3. Issuers of e-money tokens shall ensure that holders of e-money tokens are provided with a claim on the issuer: holders shall be able to redeem, at any moment and at par value, the monetary value of the e-money token held.

4. The authorisation referred to in paragraph 1 shall be valid for the entire Union and shall allow the issuer to offer e-money tokens to the public in the Union and to seek admission of such crypto-assets to trading.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Article 51 - Content and form of the crypto-asset white paper for e-money tokens

1. The crypto-asset white paper for an e-money token shall contain all of the following information:

(a) information about the issuer of the e-money token;

(b) information about the e-money token;

(c) information about the public offer of the e-money token or its admission to trading;

(d) information on the rights and obligations attached to the e-money token;

(e) information on the underlying technology and standards used;

(f) information on the risks;

(g) information on the principal adverse impacts on the climate and other environment-related adverse impacts of the consensus mechanism.

2. All information in the crypto-asset white paper shall be fair, clear and not misleading.
